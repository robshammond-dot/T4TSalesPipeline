Reference: how this pipeline was decided

UDMS mapping
UDMS has more stages than this for managing the operational side of a booking (hotel confirmations, itinerary building, and the rest), but those aren't relevant here since Freshsales doesn't manage the post-sale booking process the way UDMS does. Only three UDMS statuses matter for this pipeline: Quoted, Quote Pending, and Booked. There's no integration between the two systems, so every Freshsales stage is moved manually, which is exactly why this pipeline stays close to those three names rather than inventing stages nothing in UDMS reflects. Consulting Follow Up and Quoted Follow Up were removed for the same reason: the follow-up sequence runs on top of the Quoted stage itself, using a sequence condition tied to the stage rather than a separate stage for "following up", so a dedicated Follow Up stage added manual admin without adding function. Freshsales' job stops being about matching UDMS once a deal is Won, UDMS owns the operational booking from there, but Freshsales still handles post-booking communication and any pre-trip journey information shared with the traveller, so Won isn't the end of the relationship, just the end of the sales pipeline tracking it.
Quoted vs Quote Pending
UDMS's Quote Pending covers everything from "quote sent, waiting" through to "client is filling in the booking form and passport info." Freshsales' Quoted stage deliberately spans that whole window too, the follow-up sequence keeps nudging until something changes. Booking Acceptance and Payment is the manual signal that something has changed: the moment the agent sees the booking form and passport info come through, they move the deal out of Quoted specifically to stop the sequence firing at someone who's already progressing.
Assignment
Round robin is native and confirmed (Freshsales Auto-assignment rules). Skill-scoped round robin, routing by destination or specialism, is buildable the same way, using multiple rules each scoped to a smaller group. Workload/capacity-based assignment is not confirmed available in Freshsales; that would need checking with Freshworks directly before it's offered as an option.
Deal stage on Sequences
Sequences only run against Contacts and Accounts, never Deals directly, so a sequence step can't condition on a Deal's pipeline stage on its own. The fix is a single Contact field ("Status") kept in sync by one workflow that watches the Deal's stage and copies it across whenever it changes, not a second stage list mirroring the whole pipeline. A full mirrored pipeline would still need the same bridge, just with more fields to keep in sync for no real benefit, since it's one enquiry per customer at a time here. The edge case (a customer with two open deals at once, a live Won booking and a fresh enquiry) is real but rare enough not to engineer around directly; a saved view flagging contacts with more than one open Deal is the practical safety net, a human checks it rather than the field trying to hold two truths at once.

Reference: Auto-Assignment Example, New Stage

Auto-assignment means nobody has to sit there manually assigning every new enquiry as it lands, Freshsales does it the moment a lead comes in. That doesn't mean losing control over who gets what: each rule defines exactly which consultants are eligible, the team decides the group, Freshsales just handles the turn-taking inside it. Rules run in priority order, set directly in Freshsales' Auto-assignment rules screen, so the more specific rule needs the higher priority or the catch-all below it grabs everything first.

Rule 1Priority 1, runs first
Thailand / Asia specialists
Condition: Destination contains "Thailand" or "Asia". Round-robins only among the smaller group of consultants who specialise in that region, so an Asia enquiry always lands with someone who actually knows it.
Rule 2Priority 2, catch-all
All other new enquiries
Condition: none, catches anything Rule 1 didn't. Round-robins across the full consulting team, cycling back to the start once everyone's had a turn.
On enquiry load: round robin naturally spreads new enquiries evenly, nobody gets skipped over or over-picked based on who happens to be top of mind. Workload/capacity-based assignment is not available in Freshsales, that's a Freshdesk feature for support tickets, not something Deals has access to. Practical middle ground: keep round robin for the fairness it already gives you, and check deal counts per consultant periodically via reporting, adjusting who's in each rule's rotation if someone's consistently carrying more than their share. More specialisms just mean more rules stacked above the catch-all, each scoped to its own smaller group.

Reference: Sales Sequence Example, Consulting Stage Follow-up

Shorter and lighter than the Quoted sequence, deliberately. There's no quote yet at this stage, nothing time-sensitive to reference, and the ask is small (a few details, not a decision), so two gentle touches over five days does the job without it feeling like a chase for something that doesn't exist yet.

Day 2Status is Consulting, no reply to initial outreach
{{contact.first_name}}, just a couple of details for your Thailand trip
Hi {{contact.first_name}}, thanks for getting in touch about Thailand! To put together the right quote for you, I just need a bit more detail, who's travelling, your ideal dates, and roughly what you'd like to spend. Reply whenever suits, and I'll get started.
Day 5if not replied to Step 1, final step
Still keen to help plan your Thailand trip, {{contact.first_name}}?
No rush at all, just wanted to check in. If it's easier, feel free to WhatsApp me or give me a call, whatever's quickest for you. Looking forward to hearing from you when you're ready.
Exits sequence when: customer replies, or email status is Bounced. No third step, and no auto-move to Lost or Parked after Day 5, if there's still no reply, that's a judgement call for the agent to make manually, not something the sequence decides on its own.

Reference: Sales Sequence Example, Quoted Stage Follow-up

A Classic sequence, kept deliberately simple: one generic Quoted follow-up, not split by destination or package type. That's a day-one choice, not a limitation, splitting by destination only earns its complexity once there's more than one Quoted sequence running and something to keep them from overlapping. Right now there isn't, so the entry condition stays a single line.

EntryContact-level condition
Status is Quoted
"Status" here is a field on the Contact, not the Deal, Sequences can only see Contacts and Accounts, never Deals directly. This field is kept accurate by a single workflow that watches the Deal's pipeline stage and copies it across whenever it changes, so the Contact always reflects whatever the Deal's actual stage is.
Day 3Status is Quoted, AND last contacted mode is not Incoming email
{{contact.first_name}}, your Thailand holiday is ready to book
Hi {{contact.first_name}}, just checking you've had a chance to look over your Thailand quote, 3 nights in Phuket and 2 in Bangkok, exactly as we discussed. Let me know if you'd like any changes, or if you're ready to lock it in.
Day 8if not replied to Step 1, AND last contacted mode is not Incoming email
Still thinking about Thailand, {{contact.first_name}}?
No pressure at all, just checking in. If budget or dates are the sticking point, I can take another look and see what flexibility there is. Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier than email.
Day 13if not replied to Step 1, AND last contacted mode is not Incoming email, final step
Last call to confirm your Thailand booking, {{contact.first_name}}
Your quote is due to expire shortly, and flight pricing can shift after that. If Thailand's still on the cards, let's get it locked in this week, reply here or give me a call and I'll take care of the rest.
Why the "not Incoming email" guard matters: "if not replied to Step 1" only catches replies to this sequence's own emails. If the traveller emails in about something else entirely, calls, or messages through another channel, that's a real conversation already happening, and none of these three steps should fire on top of it. The guard checks activity broadly, not just replies to this specific thread.
Exits sequence when: customer replies, or email status is Bounced. Moving the deal to Booking Acceptance and Payment, Parked, or Lost updates the mirrored Status field away from Quoted, which is the real off-switch, the sequence's own exit rules are a backstop, not the primary mechanism.
